Introduction

Thailand the land of the Tai People. The meaning of "tai" is uncertain, but may originally have meant "human beings," "people," or free people." In the mid-13th century two unified Thai kingdoms emerged the Sukhothai Kingdom, located in the south-central plains and the The Thai Lan Na Kingdom was established in the north with its capital at Chang Mai. The Ayutthaya Kingdom (14th-18th centuries) succeeded the Sukhothai and would become known as the Siamese Kingdom. Western pressure in the 1890s and 1900s led to Siam signing trade treaties that reduced the countrys sovereignty and independence.

Following a bloodless revolution in 1932 that led to the establishment of a constitutional monarchy, Thailand's political history was marked by a series of mostly bloodless coups with power concentrated among military and bureaucratic elites. Thailand became a US treaty ally in 1954

In the 21st century, Thailand has experienced additional turmoil, including a military coup in 2006 The military-affiliated National Council for Peace and Order (NCPO) ruled the country under PRAYUT for more than four years, drafting a new constitution that allowed the military to appoint the entire 250-member Senate and required a joint meeting of the House and Senate to select the prime minister -- which effectively gave the military a veto on the selection. The reformist Move Forward Party won the most seats in the 2023 election but was unable to form a government, and Srettha THRAVISIN from the Pheu Thai Party replaced PRAYUT as prime minister after forming a coalition of moderate and conservative parties.

Geography

Thailand's geography is marked by a rich diversity that includes mountains, fertile plains, and stunning coastlines. Situated in Southeast Asia, the country is bordered by Myanmar and Laos to the north, Cambodia to the east, and Malaysia to the south, with the Andaman Sea to the west and the Gulf of Thailand to the south.

People & Society

The people of Thailand, known as Thais, are renowned for their warmth and hospitality, which is reflected in the cultural concept of "sanuk," emphasizing enjoyment and happiness in daily life. Thailand's population is ethnically diverse, with the majority being ethnic Thai, alongside significant communities of Chinese, Malays, and indigenous groups.

Religions

The country is predominantly Buddhist, with the religion playing a central role in daily life and cultural practices. Traditional festivals, such as Songkran and Loy Krathong, showcase the rich cultural heritage and strong community ties. Overall, the vibrant customs, languages, and lifestyles of the Thai people contribute to a unique and colorful societal fabric.

Demographic profile

Thailand has experienced a substantial fertility decline since the 1960s largely due to the nationwide success of its voluntary family planning program.Thailands receptiveness to family planning reflects the predominant faith, Theravada Buddhism, which emphasizes individualism, personal responsibility, and independent decision-making
